## China Routing: Understanding the Differences

This is where things get nuanced, and it matters a lot if you're serving mainland Chinese audiences.

The **Hong Kong HGC business broadband line** has different routing paths depending on carrier:

- **China Mobile**: Direct routing both ways. Tested throughput: 79–105 Mbps upload, latency around 44–74ms. This is the star performer.
- **China Unicom**: Routes through South Korea via KT. Latency adds 40–90ms, but throughput stays reasonable at 78–91 Mbps.
- **China Telecom**: Also via South Korea (KT). The most variable — uploads ranged from 19 to 104 Mbps depending on time and conditions. Downloads held steadier at 76–93 Mbps.

If your audience is primarily on mobile networks (China Mobile has the largest subscriber base in mainland China), the HGC line performs exceptionally well. If you need consistent performance across all three major carriers, the Japan Tokyo Premium IIJ package is the better call — pricier, but the China-optimised routing through IIJ delivers far more predictable results.

## What to Watch Out For

A few limitations worth knowing upfront:

**Manual activation on some packages** — Starlink VPS and certain residential packages require support ticket activation rather than instant provisioning. If you need a server online in minutes, check whether your chosen package is auto-provisioned first.

**Residential plans have usage restrictions** — No P2P traffic (BitTorrent etc.), and banking websites are blocked per ISP acceptable use policies. These are ISP-level rules, not arbitrary RadoNet policy, but they're real constraints.

**Support is via Telegram and email** — No phone support. Response times are generally reasonable for technical issues, but if you expect immediate phone escalation, adjust expectations.

**No managed services** — You're administering your own Linux server. No cPanel, no managed backups, no guided setup. Perfectly fine for sysadmins; potentially frustrating for beginners.

**Company age** — Founded around 2022. Not a decades-old institution. For critical business infrastructure, having a backup plan is wise.
